# DevOps Roadmap 2026 — The 5 Growth Factors

![DevOps Roadmap 2026 — Intro](Intro.png)

> Right now, in 2026, there is a window of opportunity in DevOps that will not stay open forever. Companies are hiring faster than engineers are leveling up. If you move now and move smart, you can land a DevOps role — or jump from junior to senior — in months, not years.

---

## The Opportunity Window

The DevOps market is not slowing down. It is accelerating — and shifting.

Here is what changed:
- **Cloud spending continues to grow rapidly.** Every company needs people who can manage infrastructure at scale.
- **Kubernetes became the default.** Not "one of the options." The default.
- **Security is no longer a separate team's job.** DevSecOps is now expected from every engineer who touches infrastructure.
- **Infrastructure as Code is non-negotiable.** If you are clicking through consoles, you are already behind.
- **AI rewrote the daily workflow.** Engineers who use AI tools effectively are measurably more productive. Those who do not are falling behind.
- **Observability became a distinct discipline.** SLOs, error budgets, and distributed tracing are no longer advanced topics — they are baseline expectations for any production system.

These are the 6 growth factors. Master them in the right order, and you become the engineer companies are fighting to hire.

> "6 factors. Right order. That is the entire strategy."

## Course Structure

This is not a "watch and forget" course. It is a system.

**Each factor module gives you:**
1. **Why it matters** — so you understand the market, not just the tool
2. **What to learn** — the core skills, ranked by ROI
3. **What NOT to learn first** — so you stop wasting months on low-value detours
4. **Depth levels** — beginner, strong, expert — so you know when to stop and move on
5. **How AI changes the game** — practical workflows you can use today
6. **Common traps** — mistakes that cost people 6-12 months
7. **Exercises** — real projects, not toy examples
8. **Job-ready signals** — a checklist so you know when you are actually ready

**Plus two critical guides:**
- [90-roadmap](./90-roadmap/) — The full learning path: phases, timelines, skill dependencies, portfolio projects
- [91-mistakes](./91-mistakes/) — The 10 mistakes that keep people stuck for years

## How to Use This Course

### If you are starting from zero:
1. Read the [Roadmap](./90-roadmap/) first — it gives you the phase-by-phase plan
2. Read the [Mistakes](./91-mistakes/) guide — so you do not repeat what everyone else does wrong
3. Go through factors 1-5 in order — they build on each other
4. Do every exercise — reading without doing is entertainment, not learning

### If you already have some experience:
1. Skim the [Roadmap](./90-roadmap/) to find your current phase
2. Jump to the factor where you have the biggest gap
3. Focus on the "strong" depth level — that is where the job market pays
4. Use the job-ready checklists to identify what is missing

### If you are preparing for interviews:
1. Check the job-ready signals in each factor — those are interview topics
2. Build at least 2 portfolio projects from the [Roadmap](./90-roadmap/)
3. Pay special attention to [DevSecOps](./03-devsecops/) and [IaC](./04-infrastructure-as-code/) — these are where most candidates fall short

> The biggest mistake I see is people spending a year learning in circles. This roadmap is designed to get you from where you are to where you need to be — in a straight line. Let us get into it.
